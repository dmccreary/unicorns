# Course Description: Unicorns and Other Mythical Beasts

**Title:** Unicorns and Other Mythical Beasts

**Subtitle:** An Essential Guide to Creatures That Definitely Exist and the Technologies That Will Improve Them

**Course Number:** MYTH-401 / AI-666

## Credit Hours

3 credit hours (meets the General Education requirement for Magical Thinking)

## Target Audience

This course is designed for a general audience, ages 10 and up (5th grade through adult learners). No prior experience with unicorns, dragons, or artificial intelligence is required, though a willingness to suspend disbelief is strongly encouraged. The course is particularly relevant for:

- Students who have been told "AI won't affect your job"
- Educators who believe worksheets will never become obsolete
- Middle managers who just learned what ChatGPT is
- Anyone who has ever said "that's not how we do things here"
- People who collect porcelain unicorn figurines (no judgment)

## Prerequisites

- Basic literacy (ability to read satirical content without calling the authorities)
- A functioning sense of humor
- Willingness to confront uncomfortable truths disguised as fantasy
- Zero prior knowledge of unicorns (we will teach you everything you need to know, and more)

## Course Overview

We live in an age of unprecedented transformation. Artificial intelligence is reshaping every industry, redefining every profession, and generating an alarming number of textbooks about itself. And yet, despite these sweeping changes, society remains woefully underprepared in one critical area: **unicorn literacy**.

The unicorn — a creature of extraordinary beauty, unverified existence, and surprisingly robust venture capital valuations — has been a cornerstone of human civilization for millennia. From the ancient cave paintings of Lascaux (if you squint) to the billion-dollar startup ecosystem of Silicon Valley, unicorns have shaped our economies, our mythologies, and our Instagram feeds.

This course provides a rigorous, evidence-adjacent exploration of unicorns and their mythical companions, while simultaneously examining the parallel universe of artificial intelligence — a field that, like unicorns, promises magical solutions to all of humanity's problems and may or may not actually deliver on those promises.

Through a carefully curated collection of satirical stories, interactive simulations, and graphic novels, students will develop critical thinking skills, media literacy, and the ability to distinguish between things that are real, things that are imaginary, and things that are "in beta."

**Why is this course essential?** Because in a world where AI can generate a 1,000-page interactive textbook with MicroSims in an afternoon, someone needs to ask whether it *should*. And because unicorns are delightful.

## Course Format

This is an interactive intelligent textbook — yes, another one — featuring:

- **Satirical Stories:** A collection of short fiction and creative nonfiction that uses mythical beasts as allegories for modern technological disruption
- **Graphic Novels:** Approximately 12 illustrated short narratives exploring the human side of AI anxiety
- **MicroSims:** Interactive simulations and infographics built with web technologies for hands-on exploration
- **Quizzes**: Because we must assess whether you truly understand unicorns
- **A Glossary:** Defining terms you never knew you needed, like "hornification" and "mythical product-market fit"
- **A FAQ:** Frequently asked questions about the impact of Unicorns on society and how AI can write better satire than humans

## Topics Covered

### Unit 1: The Foundation of Unicorn Studies (Chapters 1–3)

1. **A Brief and Totally Accurate History of Unicorns** — From ancient Mesopotamia to modern venture capital, tracing the unicorn's journey through human civilization. Includes a timeline of key unicorn milestones that definitely happened.

2. **Taxonomy of Mythical Beasts** — A rigorous classification system for creatures that do not exist, including dragons, griffins, phoenixes, centaurs, and AI systems that work exactly as demonstrated in the sales pitch.

3. **The Unicorn-Industrial Complex** — How unicorns became a $4.7 trillion metaphor and why every startup wants to be one. Explores the disturbing overlap between magical thinking and Series B funding.

### Unit 2: AI and the Myth of Progress (Chapters 4–7)

4. **The Emperor's New Algorithm** — A satirical exploration of AI hype cycles, from "this changes everything" to "we're pivoting to blockchain." Based on true events. All of them.

5. **Deer in the Headlights: When Disruption Comes Knocking** — Why people freeze when confronted with technological change, and why "we've always done it this way" is not a viable survival strategy. Accompanied by a graphic novel.

6. **The Ostrich Academy: A School That Refuses to See AI** — A fictional (or is it?) institution where administrators have banned all discussion of artificial intelligence and replaced computer labs with calligraphy studios. Students thrive. Briefly.

7. **The Job Thief: A Dragon's Tale** — The story of a well-meaning dragon named Algorithm who keeps accidentally automating everyone's jobs while trying to be helpful. A sympathetic villain origin story.

### Unit 3: Mythical Beasts in Modern Society (Chapters 8–11)

8. **Centaur Workforce: Half Human, Half Machine** — The emerging reality of human-AI collaboration, told through the lens of creatures who have always been half-and-half and are frankly tired of the metaphor.

9. **Phoenix Rising: The Art of Reinvention** — Stories of industries and individuals who adapted to technological change by bursting into flames and emerging as something new. Not all of them survived.

10. **The Siren Song of Automation** — How the promise of "set it and forget it" lures organizations onto the rocks. Features cautionary tales from companies that automated their customer service and lost their customers.

11. **Unicorn Spotting: Separating Fact from Fantasy in Tech Claims** — A practical guide to identifying exaggerated claims about AI capabilities, featuring real quotes that sound fake and fake quotes that sound terrifyingly real.

### Unit 4: The Future of Mythical Intelligence (Chapters 12–14)

12. **Breeding Better Unicorns with AI** — A speculative exploration of how generative AI could design the perfect mythical creature. Includes interactive simulations of AI-enhanced unicorns with configurable features like rocket hooves and WiFi-enabled horns.

13. **The Grand Council of Mythical Beasts** — A fictional summit where all mythical creatures gather to discuss the AI threat to their livelihoods. Even imaginary beings are worried about being replaced by AI-generated content.

14. **The Last Textbook** — A meta-fictional conclusion in which this textbook becomes self-aware and writes its own sequel. The author is both relieved and deeply concerned.

### Unit 5: Unicorns and Other Technology Fantasies (Chapters 15–18)

A comparative study of unicorns and the technology promises that share their most defining characteristic: everyone talks about them, venture capitalists fund them, and nobody has actually seen one work at scale.

15. **Economically Viable Quantum Computing: The Unicorn of Physics** — For decades, quantum computing has been "five years away" — a timeline suspiciously similar to the one unicorn breeders have cited since the Middle Ages. This chapter compares the breathless promise of quantum supremacy to the breathless promise of a horse with a horn, examining how both have attracted billions in funding based largely on the argument that "it would be really cool if it worked." Features interviews with fictional quantum physicists who are "cautiously optimistic" in exactly the same tone as medieval unicorn hunters.

16. **Efficient Blockchain: The Distributed Ledger of Narnia** — Blockchain was supposed to revolutionize everything from banking to banana tracking. Instead, it revolutionized the electricity bill. This chapter explores the parallels between blockchain's promise of trustless, decentralized efficiency and the unicorn's promise of purity and grace — both beautiful in theory, both requiring you to ignore some inconvenient facts about thermodynamics. Includes a side-by-side comparison of energy consumed by Bitcoin mining versus energy consumed by a dragon, and the dragon wins on efficiency.

17. **Ethical Bitcoin: A Contradiction in Horns** — Can Bitcoin be ethical? Can a unicorn be ordinary? This chapter examines the heroic efforts to make cryptocurrency environmentally sustainable and socially responsible, drawing uncomfortable parallels to attempts to domesticate mythical beasts. Features the tale of a well-meaning unicorn ranch that promised "free-range, organic, carbon-neutral magic" and collapsed when someone checked the math. Also covers the recurring phenomenon of crypto founders who promise to "bank the unbanked" while unbanking the already-banked.

18. **The Bestiary of Vaporware: A Field Guide** — A comprehensive taxonomy comparing mythical creatures to mythical technologies. Self-driving cars are the griffins of Silicon Valley — half eagle, half lion, fully unable to handle a left turn in the rain. The metaverse is the kraken — enormous, tentacled, and currently at the bottom of the ocean. Fusion power is the phoenix — perpetually about to rise from the ashes, any decade now. This chapter provides a handy identification chart so students can quickly classify any new technology announcement as "real," "aspirational," "delusional," or "unicorn."

## Graphic Novels

The course features approximately 12 short graphic novels (4–8 pages each) that provide visual storytelling companions to the chapter content:

1. **Deer in the Headlights** — A deer sees the truck of AI progress barreling toward it and decides to form a committee to study the situation.
2. **The Ostrich Principal** — A school administrator buries their head in the sand while students build an AI tutor in the classroom next door.
3. **Dragon Layoffs** — A corporate dragon must inform the village that their positions have been "reimagined" by a chatbot.
4. **The Unicorn Startup** — A unicorn pitches investors on a product that is, literally, magic. They fund it immediately.
5. **The Mermaid's Resume** — A mermaid tries to update her LinkedIn profile to include "AI-adjacent" skills she does not possess.
6. **Griffin IT Support** — A griffin tries to explain to a medieval kingdom why they need to update their defenses to AI-powered siege equipment.
7. **The Phoenix Retraining Program** — A phoenix enrolls in a coding bootcamp after being told that "rising from the ashes" is no longer a marketable skill.
8. **Centaur Performance Review** — A centaur receives feedback that their "human half" needs improvement, but their "horse half" exceeds expectations.
9. **Minotaur's Maze of Bureaucracy** — A minotaur navigates a labyrinth of institutional resistance to adopting new technology.
10. **The Cyclops Data Analyst** — A cyclops is hired for data analysis because "one eye is all you need for tunnel vision."
11. **Pegasus Delivery Service** — A pegasus delivery company is disrupted by drones and must pivot to "artisanal, hooves-on delivery."
12. **The Kraken Influencer** — A kraken becomes a social media influencer promoting AI tools, but no one can tell if the tentacles are real or AI-generated.

## MicroSims (Interactive Simulations and Infographics)

The course includes several interactive browser-based simulations:

1. **Timeline of Unicorns in History** — An interactive timeline infographic tracing unicorn sightings, references, and cultural significance from 3000 BCE to present day. Users can scroll through eras, click on events, and read detailed (and increasingly absurd) descriptions. *(Interactive Infographic Overlay)*

2. **AI Unicorn Feature Generator** — An interactive simulation where users configure an AI-enhanced unicorn by selecting features (horn type, wing configuration, data processing speed, WiFi range). The simulation generates a visual representation and a "venture capital valuation" based on selected features. *(Interactive Infographic Overlay)*

3. **Mythical Beast Job Displacement Calculator** — Users input a mythical creature's job title and skills, and the simulation calculates the probability that an AI system will replace them, complete with a "threat level" visualization and sarcastic career advice. *(Interactive Infographic Overlay)*

4. **The Hype Cycle Roller Coaster** — An interactive infographic showing the classic Gartner Hype Cycle, but populated with mythical beasts at each stage. Users can drag creatures between stages and read commentary about technology adoption patterns. *(Interactive Infographic Overlay)*

5. **Unicorn Population Dynamics** — A simulation modeling the population of unicorns over time, affected by variables like "belief level," "Instagram exposure," and "venture capital funding." Users adjust sliders to see how unicorn populations rise and fall. *(p5.js MicroSim)*

6. **The Great Mythical Debate** — An interactive Venn diagram showing the overlap between "Things AI Can Do," "Things Unicorns Can Do," and "Things People Claim AI Can Do." Users can click on intersections to reveal humorous examples. *(Interactive Infographic Overlay)*

7. **The Bestiary of Vaporware: Interactive Field Guide** — An interactive classification chart where users drag technology announcements onto a spectrum from "Shipping Product" to "Pure Unicorn." Features real and fictional examples of quantum computing breakthroughs, blockchain revolutions, ethical crypto initiatives, self-driving cars, and fusion power — each with a mythical beast counterpart, a "years until delivery" countdown that never reaches zero, and a running tally of venture capital consumed. *(Interactive Infographic Overlay)*

## Learning Objectives (2001 Bloom's Taxonomy)

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

### Level 1: Remember

1. **List** at least five types of mythical beasts and their distinguishing characteristics
2. **Identify** key milestones in the (fictional) history of unicorns
3. **Recall** the stages of the technology hype cycle using mythical beast examples
4. **Define** key vocabulary terms including "unicorn," "disruption," "hallucination" (both AI and magical varieties), and "mythical product-market fit"
5. **Name** three ways AI is currently used in education (real ones, not the made-up ones from Chapter 6)

### Level 2: Understand

6. **Explain** the allegorical relationship between mythical beasts and modern technology trends
7. **Summarize** the main arguments for and against AI adoption in education, using examples from the satirical stories
8. **Describe** common human responses to technological disruption as illustrated by the "Deer in the Headlights" phenomenon
9. **Interpret** satirical content to identify the underlying real-world commentary
10. **Paraphrase** complex AI concepts using mythical beast analogies that a 5th grader could understand

### Level 3: Apply

11. **Demonstrate** the ability to use interactive simulations to explore "what-if" scenarios in unicorn population dynamics
12. **Use** the AI Unicorn Feature Generator to design a mythical creature optimized for a specific (absurd) purpose
13. **Calculate** the theoretical job displacement risk for a mythical creature using the interactive calculator
14. **Illustrate** a technology adoption challenge using a mythical beast allegory of the student's own creation
15. **Operate** interactive timeline and infographic tools to investigate historical claims about unicorns

### Level 4: Analyze

16. **Distinguish** between satirical claims and factual statements about AI capabilities
17. **Compare** the responses of different characters (mythical and human) to technological disruption
18. **Categorize** AI claims into "verified," "plausible," "optimistic," and "unicorn-level fantasy"
19. **Examine** the parallels between historical resistance to new technologies and current AI skepticism
20. **Deconstruct** a startup pitch to identify which claims are supported by evidence and which are supported by wishful thinking
21. **Differentiate** between genuinely emerging technologies (AI, robotics) and perpetually emerging technologies (quantum computing, ethical crypto, fusion power) using the Unicorn Classification Framework

### Level 5: Evaluate

22. **Assess** the validity of common arguments against AI adoption in education
23. **Judge** whether a given AI application is genuinely useful or merely impressive-looking
24. **Critique** the narrative strategies used in the graphic novels to convey complex ideas through humor
25. **Defend** a position on the responsible use of AI in creative industries, using evidence from the course materials
26. **Appraise** the effectiveness of satire as a tool for public education about technology

### Level 6: Create

27. **Design** an original mythical beast that serves as an allegory for a current AI trend
28. **Compose** a short satirical story about technology adoption using the narrative techniques studied in the course
29. **Construct** a presentation that uses humor and mythical beast metaphors to explain a real AI concept to a general audience
30. **Propose** a balanced strategy for AI adoption in a school or workplace, informed by the cautionary tales in the course
31. **Produce** an original graphic novel panel or storyboard that satirizes a current event in technology
32. **Create** an entry for the Bestiary of Vaporware, classifying a real technology announcement using the unicorn taxonomy from Unit 5

## Assessment Strategy

- **Unicorn Identification Quizzes** (20%) — Multiple-choice and matching assessments on chapter content
- **Satirical Analysis Essays** (20%) — Short written analyses of the allegorical content
- **MicroSim Exploration Reports** (20%) — Guided activities using the interactive simulations
- **Creative Mythical Beast Project** (25%) — Original satirical creation (story, graphic novel, or simulation concept)
- **The Final Exam: Real or Fake?** (15%) — Students are presented with 50 statements about AI and must determine which are true, which are satirical, and which are direct quotes from actual press releases (the hardest category)

## A Note on Truth and Fiction

Some of the stories in this textbook are based on real events. Some are complete fantasy. Some are based on real events that are so absurd they read like fantasy. And some are fantasy that has since become reality. We leave it as an exercise for the reader to determine which is which. Good luck. You'll need it.
