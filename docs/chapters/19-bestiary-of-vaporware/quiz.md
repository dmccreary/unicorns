# Quiz: The Bestiary of Vaporware — A Field Guide

Test your ability to classify any technology announcement as real, aspirational, delusional, or unicorn. This is the final exam. The beasts are watching.

---

#### 1. Self-driving cars are classified as which mythical beast?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. The unicorn — funded on belief, no verified specimens at scale
2. The griffin — half perception system, half decision system, struggling with integration
3. The phoenix — repeatedly dying and claiming rebirth
4. The siren — singing a beautiful song that leads to shipwreck
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. Self-driving cars are griffins: half eagle (perception system that sees everything) and half lion (decision system that acts with confidence). Each half is impressive independently. The integration is where things get difficult, "particularly in novel conditions" — rain, snow, construction zones, and the human ability to create situations faster than any AI can be trained on them.

    **Concept Tested:** Self-Driving Car

#### 2. The gap between Level 4 and Level 5 driving automation is analogous to what?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. The gap between a horse and a unicorn
2. The gap between a griffin that can fly in clear weather and one that can fly in hurricanes
3. The gap between a demo and a product
4. The gap between a committee's first meeting and its forty-seventh
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. Level 4 (high automation in defined areas) works in specific, mapped, favorable conditions. Level 5 (full automation everywhere) requires handling every conceivable scenario. The first 90% of the problem took a decade. The remaining 10% includes rain, snow, construction, unusual obstacles, aggressive drivers, and "the human ability to create novel situations faster than any AI can be trained on them." That 10% may take another decade — or may prove intractable.

    **Concept Tested:** Self-Driving Car

#### 3. Fusion power is classified as which mythical beast?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. The unicorn — perpetually almost real
2. The dragon — destructive but powerful
3. The phoenix — perpetually dying and claiming rebirth, on a seventy-year cycle
4. The kraken — enormous and mostly submerged
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. Fusion power has been "dying and being reborn" on a regular cycle since the 1950s. Each generation achieves genuine progress, celebrates it, and predicts commercial viability within the researcher's remaining career. The prediction is always optimistic. The progress is always real. The timeline is always wrong. The joke in the physics community: "Fusion is always 30 years away."

    **Concept Tested:** Fusion Power

#### 4. According to the Bestiary of Vaporware table, which technology is classified as "Pure Unicorn" on the vaporware spectrum?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Quantum Computing
2. Self-Driving Cars (Level 5)
3. The Metaverse
4. AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **D**. AGI is classified as Pure Unicorn: "Everyone talks about it; nobody has seen it." Its five-year prognosis is "Still five years away." Quantum computing and fusion power are classified as "Works in Lab." Self-driving cars (Level 5) and ethical Bitcoin are "Aspirational." The metaverse is in the "Trough." AGI alone occupies the mythical category where the technology's primary product is prediction rather than performance.

    **Concept Tested:** Self-Driving Car

#### 5. The field guide's three-step diagnostic process for evaluating any technology announcement consists of which steps?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Read the press release, check the stock price, invest
2. Identify the claim, assign a beast, assign a vaporware level
3. Form a committee, request more data, defer decision
4. Apply the Turing Test, check the benchmark, contact the vendor
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. The Bestiary of Vaporware provides a practical, repeatable tool: (1) identify the technology claim, (2) assign a mythical beast (which creature does the technology most resemble?), (3) assign a vaporware level (Shipping Product to Pure Unicorn). A fourth diagnostic step asks the Unicorn Test: "Would this assessment change if I replaced the technology name with 'unicorn'?"

    **Concept Tested:** Self-Driving Car

#### 6. What distinguishes fusion power from other technology fantasies in this unit?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. It has received the least investment of any technology fantasy
2. If it succeeds, the payoff is genuinely unlimited clean energy — stakes high enough to justify patience
3. It has already been commercially deployed in three countries
4. It does not follow the five years away pattern
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. Unlike quantum computing (uncertain physics), blockchain (uncertain demand), or the metaverse (no demand), fusion's challenge is known: confining plasma at 100 million degrees Celsius in a net-energy-positive reaction at competitive cost. The difference is that if fusion succeeds, the payoff is genuinely unlimited clean energy. "The stakes justify the patience. The timeline justifies the skepticism."

    **Concept Tested:** Fusion Power

#### 7. Sparkle observes that a self-driving car operating in Phoenix, Arizona, on pre-mapped roads with a remote operator on standby, is comparable to what?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. A fully autonomous vehicle ready for mass deployment
2. A Level 5 self-driving car with minor limitations
3. A teenager with a learner's permit
4. A breakthrough that renders human drivers obsolete
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. Sparkle notes that a car driving itself under specific conditions (clear day, pre-mapped roads, remote operator standing by) is "an impressive achievement. It is not a self-driving car. It is a car that drives itself under specific conditions, which is also a description of a teenager with a learner's permit." The gap between conditional autonomy and full autonomy is the gap where the griffin struggles with integration.

    **Concept Tested:** Self-Driving Car

#### 8. The chapter describes the final diagnostic question from the Unicorn Test. What is it?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. "Has this technology received venture capital funding?"
2. "Does this technology have a Wikipedia page?"
3. "Would this assessment change if I replaced the technology name with 'unicorn'?"
4. "Has this technology been endorsed by a minimum of three LinkedIn thought leaders?"
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. If you can substitute "unicorn" for the technology name and the sentence still makes sense, the technology may not exist. "A transformative breakthrough in unicorn intelligence that will revolutionize every industry" and "A transformative breakthrough in quantum intelligence that will revolutionize every industry" are equally plausible and equally unsupported. The test exploits the structural identity between mythology and marketing.

#### 9. Tesla's "Full Self-Driving" capability is described in the chapter with what critical observation?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. It has achieved Level 5 autonomy across all conditions
2. The name implies the car can drive itself; the fine print says otherwise
3. It is the most advanced self-driving system currently available
4. It was voluntarily recalled and renamed to "Partial Self-Driving"
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. Tesla announced "Full Self-Driving" as a purchasable option in 2016. The name implies the car can drive itself. The fine print, legal disclaimers, and actual capability say otherwise — it remains a Level 2 system requiring the driver's hands on the wheel and eyes on the road. The gap between the product name and the product capability is the griffin problem in its purest commercial form.

    **Concept Tested:** Self-Driving Car

#### 10. The textbook's final observation is that the analytical tools it provides are "not tools for cynicism." What are they tools for?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Dismissing every technology as fantasy
2. Generating more effective pitch decks
3. Clarity — distinguishing technologies that work from technologies that merely promise to
4. Winning arguments on social media about AI
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. Beast classification, vaporware taxonomy, hype cycle analysis, media literacy, critical thinking, unicorn spotting, and siren song recognition are tools for clarity, not cynicism. "The goal is not to dismiss every technology as fantasy. The goal is to distinguish the technologies that work from the technologies that merely promise to, using evidence rather than enthusiasm as the criterion." Some unicorns are real. The giant squid was mythical until it was photographed.
