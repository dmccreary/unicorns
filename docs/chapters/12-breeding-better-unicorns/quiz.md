# Quiz: Breeding Better Unicorns with AI

Test your understanding of why a WiFi-enabled horn is more fundable than a better horse.

#### 1. The appeal to novelty is best described as which logical fallacy?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Assuming something is better because it is older and more established
2. Assuming something is better simply because it is newer
3. Assuming something is better because a famous person endorsed it
4. Assuming something is better because it costs more
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. The appeal to novelty assumes newer means better — the foundational argument of technology marketing. The chapter observes: "Version 4.0 changes everything. Version 3.0 also changed everything. The total amount of change should, by now, be approximately everything cubed." In an economy that rewards innovation over utility, a WiFi-enabled horn is more fundable than a better horse.

    **Concept Tested:** Appeal to Novelty

#### 3. Interactive simulations are described as powerful learning tools because they support which of the following?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Only the Remember level of Bloom's Taxonomy
2. Only the Create level of Bloom's Taxonomy
3. All six levels of Bloom's Taxonomy simultaneously
4. No levels of Bloom's Taxonomy, as they are purely entertainment
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. Interactive simulations support all six Bloom's levels: visual elements create memorable associations (Remember), cause-and-effect is observable (Understand), students manipulate parameters (Apply), compare outcomes across configurations (Analyze), assess which configurations produce better results (Evaluate), and design novel configurations (Create). The unicorn simulator is absurd. The learning mechanism is real.

    **Concept Tested:** Interactive Simulation

#### 4. The chapter describes the intelligent textbook as the phoenix of what industry?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. The unicorn farming industry
2. The educational publishing industry
3. The calligraphy supply industry
4. The medieval bestiary reproduction industry
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. The traditional textbook — printed, static, updated every 3-5 years at $150 per student — is burning. The intelligent textbook rises from the ashes: cheaper, more interactive, continuously updated. Whether it is better depends on your definition: "more engaging and adaptive" — probably yes; "more carefully reviewed and error-checked" — probably no. The traditional textbook had a two-year editorial process. The intelligent textbook has an AI with a confidence interval.

    **Concept Tested:** Intelligent Textbook

#### 5. Graphic novels are included in this textbook because visual storytelling offers which advantage that text alone does not?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. They are cheaper to produce than prose chapters
2. They eliminate the need for learning objectives and Bloom's alignment
3. They provide emotional immediacy and simultaneous information channels
4. They have been proven to increase standardized test scores by 47%
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. A drawing of a deer staring at headlights conveys the freeze response more powerfully than a paragraph describing it. A single panel can show a character's expression, environment, and speech bubble simultaneously — three channels of information in one frame. The graphic novels are allegorical: the dragon conducting layoffs is your VP of Engineering, and the humor comes from recognition.

    **Concept Tested:** Visual Storytelling

#### 6. In a population dynamics model applied to unicorn startups, what does high belief combined with high funding and low reality-checking produce?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Sustainable, steady growth of the unicorn population
2. Exponential growth followed by a crash
3. A stable equilibrium where supply meets demand
4. A gradual decline as investor interest wanes
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. High belief + high funding + low reality-checking produces the "Hype Bubble" scenario: exponential growth followed by a dramatic crash when the Reality Index eventually crosses the Belief Index. The population dynamics model demonstrates that positive feedback loops (hype generates more hype) cause booms, while negative feedback loops (reality asserts itself) cause busts.

    **Concept Tested:** Population Dynamics Model

</div>

    The correct answer is **A**. Timeline visualizations make the relative speed of change visible. The gap between calculator invention and school adoption was 23 years. The acceleration is the pattern that prose cannot make obvious.

#### 10. The QuantaCorn's horn processes qubits. Sparkle identifies which practical obstacle that investors failed to ask about?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. The regulatory approval process for quantum-enabled livestock
2. The incompatibility between mammalian body temperature and quantum computing cooling requirements
3. The limited market for quantum-processed unicorn sparkle
4. The shortage of trained quantum veterinarians
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. Sparkle observes that a qubit-processing horn would require, "at minimum, a cooling system incompatible with a mammalian body temperature of 37 degrees Celsius." Quantum computers operate at temperatures near absolute zero (-273 degrees C). The investors did not ask about the cooling system. They never do. This observation applies equally to actual quantum computing investments.

    **Concept Tested:** Appeal to Novelty
