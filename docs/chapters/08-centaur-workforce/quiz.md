# Quiz: Centaur Workforce

Test your understanding of what happens when one half of you wants to collaborate and the other half wants to gallop.

---

#### 1. The centaur workforce model originated from which development in competitive chess?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. A chess grandmaster who programmed an AI to cheat on his behalf
2. Garry Kasparov inventing "Advanced Chess" where humans work alongside computers
3. The complete replacement of all human chess players by Deep Blue in 1998
4. A committee of chess players who studied AI for 47 meetings before acting
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. After losing to Deep Blue in 1997, Kasparov invented "Centaur Chess" where a human player works alongside a computer. The human provides strategic judgment and creativity; the computer provides tactical calculation and pattern database access. Together, the centaur was better than either alone. This model became the template for human-AI collaboration across industries.

    **Concept Tested:** Centaur Workforce

#### 2. On the human-AI collaboration spectrum, "AI as supervisor" means which of the following?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. The AI reviews the human's work annually and provides constructive feedback
2. The human makes all decisions while the AI executes specific tasks
3. The AI monitors, evaluates, and assigns work while the human executes tasks
4. The AI and human take turns making decisions on alternate Tuesdays
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. "AI as supervisor" describes a relationship where the AI monitors, evaluates, and assigns — like a warehouse worker following AI-optimized routes. The chapter warns that the word "collaboration" covers everything from "the human is in charge" to "the AI is in charge and the human complies." These arrangements are not equivalent, despite using the same pleasant label.

    **Concept Tested:** Human-AI Collaboration

#### 3. The augmentation-to-replacement pipeline follows four phases. What happens in Phase 2?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. AI is introduced as a tool and workers use it to do their jobs faster
2. Management notices the team produces the same output with fewer people and stops backfilling
3. Workers who cannot adapt are offered "voluntary transition" severance packages
4. The tasks augmented in Phase 1 are now fully automated
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. In Phase 2 (Efficiency Gains), management notices that the team of 20 now produces the output that previously required 30. The next hiring cycle, the team is not expanded. Attrition is not replaced. The headcount quietly drops. This is how "AI will augment, not replace" becomes replacement through a pipeline of efficiency, restructuring, and eventual automation.

    **Concept Tested:** Augmentation vs Replacement

#### 4. The middle manager dilemma is described as "structural, not personal" because of which of the following?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Middle managers are all equally qualified to implement AI
2. Both adopting and resisting AI threaten the manager's position
3. The dilemma was created by a software bug that will be patched next quarter
4. Middle managers have been formally classified as a mythical beast category
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. A middle manager who enthusiastically adopts AI and makes their team 50% more efficient has just demonstrated the team can be 50% smaller. A manager who resists AI is labeled a luddite and replaced by someone who will adopt it. Both paths lead to the same destination — the difference is the speed. Only selective, strategic adoption offers a viable but difficult path.

    **Concept Tested:** Middle Manager Dilemma

#### 5. Which of the following skills is MOST likely to become obsolete due to AI?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Judgment in ambiguous situations requiring empathy
2. Routine legal research and case law summarization
3. Physical craftsmanship requiring manual dexterity
4. Accountability for high-stakes decisions affecting human welfare
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. Routine legal research — searching case law and summarizing precedent — can be done faster by AI than by a junior associate. The skills that remain valuable share common characteristics: they involve judgment in ambiguity, understanding human emotions, physical presence, accountability that cannot be delegated, and genuinely novel creativity. A legal researcher's decade of expertise may be reduced to "checking the AI's work" in a single product cycle.

    **Concept Tested:** Skill Obsolescence

#### 6. What question does the chapter raise about AI-generated textbooks?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Whether they should be printed on recycled paper or blockchain-verified parchment
2. Whether AI understands pedagogy or merely replicates the form without comprehending the function
3. Whether students prefer reading on tablets or enchanted scrolls
4. Whether the author receives royalties or the training data does
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. Current AI can produce content that looks pedagogically sound — it uses scaffolding, examples, and progressive complexity — but it does not understand why these techniques work. It replicates the form without comprehending the function. This textbook acknowledges its own nature as evidence: "The fact that you cannot be entirely sure which sentences were written by a human and which by a machine is evidence for every argument it makes about AI limitations."

    **Concept Tested:** AI-Generated Textbook

#### 7. The future of assessment, according to the chapter, requires redesigning tests to evaluate what?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. A student's typing speed and chatbot access
2. Factual recall through multiple-choice questions
3. What AI cannot do — critical thinking, ethical judgment, and creative originality
4. The student's ability to form a committee and produce meeting minutes
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. If AI can write essays, solve problems, and answer questions, traditional tests measure the student's access to a chatbot, not the student's learning. The future requires evaluating skills AI cannot replicate: critical thinking, ethical judgment, creative originality, and the ability to ask better questions than the ones on the test. Sparkle notes: "Redesign the assessment. The chatbot is not going away."

    **Concept Tested:** Future of Assessment

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. 50,000
2. 150,000
3. 400,000

</div>

    The correct answer is **C**. #### 9. According to the collaboration spectrum, which job role faces the LEAST risk of AI replacement?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Customer service agent
2. Warehouse worker
3. Therapist
4. Data entry clerk
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. The therapist's work scores low on every dimension that drives AI replacement: it is non-routine, judgment-heavy, deeply personal, non-delegable in accountability, and requires physical presence. AI's role in therapy is "Tool" (minimal). The customer service agent, by contrast, scores high on routine, scalable, and delegable dimensions — placing it at "Replacement risk."

    **Concept Tested:** Human-AI Collaboration

#### 10. Sparkle's tip for evaluating your own performance in the AI era is to ask which question?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. "Am I using AI more than my colleagues?"
2. "Which of my outputs would survive if the AI disappeared tomorrow?"
3. "Has my manager noticed I am using AI tools?"
4. "Can I add 'AI-assisted productivity' to my LinkedIn profile?"
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. The answer to this question identifies your actual skills. Everything that would not survive the AI's disappearance is the horn — the AI's contribution attributed to you. The performance review paradox means AI-enhanced output is often evaluated as human output, making it impossible to assess whether workers are developing, stagnating, or atrophying behind a facade of improved metrics.

    **Concept Tested:** Skill Obsolescence
