# References: Centaur Workforce — Half Human, Half Machine

1. [Centaur (chess)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_chess) - Wikipedia - History of human-computer chess teams that outperform both humans and computers alone, the origin of the "centaur" collaboration metaphor this chapter extends.

2. [Human–computer interaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human%E2%80%93computer_interaction) - Wikipedia - Overview of the field studying how humans and computers work together, providing the academic foundation for the chapter's collaboration framework.

3. [Centaur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centaur) - Wikipedia - Classical mythology of the half-human, half-horse creatures, including their portrayal as both wise mentors and unruly beasts — a duality the chapter exploits.

4. Human + Machine: Reimagining Work in the Age of AI - Paul R. Daugherty and H. James Wilson - Harvard Business Review Press - Accenture researchers' framework for human-AI collaboration models, the corporate-optimist perspective this chapter both uses and gently mocks.

5. Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI - Ethan Mollick - Portfolio/Penguin - Wharton professor's practical guide to human-AI partnerships, offering real collaboration strategies behind the chapter's satirical framing.

6. [Augmented Intelligence](https://www.gartner.com/en/information-technology/glossary/augmented-intelligence) - Gartner - Industry definition distinguishing AI augmentation from AI replacement, the corporate rebranding effort this chapter treats with dry amusement.

7. [Centaur AI: Combining Human and Machine Intelligence](https://hbr.org/2021/03/ai-should-augment-human-intelligence-not-replace-it) - Harvard Business Review - Analysis of augmentation versus replacement paradigms in AI deployment, supporting the chapter's exploration of where the human half adds value.

8. [Human-AI Collaboration Research](https://hai.stanford.edu/research) - Stanford HAI - Ongoing research into effective human-AI teaming, providing empirical grounding for the chapter's taxonomy of collaboration models.

9. [The Centaur's Dilemma](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/centaurs-and-cyborgs-back-front-office) - MIT Sloan Management Review - Research on how organizations balance human judgment with AI capability, the management challenge at the heart of the chapter's metaphor.

10. [Kasparov on Human-Computer Teams](https://www.chess.com/article/view/advanced-chess) - Chess.com - Garry Kasparov's firsthand account of discovering that human-AI teams beat pure AI, the founding moment for the centaur model this chapter chronicles.
