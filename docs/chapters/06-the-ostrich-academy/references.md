# References: The Ostrich Academy

1. [Artificial intelligence in education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence_in_education) - Wikipedia - Overview of AI applications in learning, tutoring systems, and adaptive education, providing the reality that the Ostrich Academy's administrators refuse to see.

2. [Educational technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Educational_technology) - Wikipedia - History and current state of technology in education, documenting decades of adoption resistance that mirrors the chapter's fictional institution.

3. [Luddite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luddite) - Wikipedia - Historical account of organized resistance to technology in the workplace, the original template for the institutional denial this chapter satirizes.

4. Failure to Disrupt: Why Technology Alone Can't Transform Education - Justin Reich - Harvard University Press - MIT researcher's analysis of why educational technology repeatedly fails to deliver on promises, adding nuance to the chapter's satire.

5. Teaching Machines: The History of Personalized Learning - Audrey Watters - MIT Press - Critical history of ed-tech hype cycles from the 1920s through Silicon Valley, demonstrating that the Ostrich Academy has plenty of real-world precedent.

6. [UNESCO AI in Education](https://www.unesco.org/en/digital-education/artificial-intelligence) - UNESCO - International guidelines on AI in education, representing the global policy conversation the chapter's administrators are ignoring.

7. [Schools That Ban AI](https://www.edweek.org/technology/see-which-states-have-passed-ai-legislation-for-schools/2024/09) - Education Week - Reporting on real school districts' AI policies ranging from outright bans to cautious integration, blurring the line between the chapter's fiction and reality.

8. [Teachers' Attitudes Toward AI](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA956-21.html) - RAND Corporation - Survey data on educator perspectives on AI integration, quantifying the resistance patterns the chapter dramatizes.

9. [Generative AI and the Future of Education](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/education/our-insights/generative-ai-and-the-future-of-education) - McKinsey & Company - Consulting-firm analysis of AI's educational impact that manages to be simultaneously urgent and noncommittal, perfectly embodying institutional fence-sitting.

10. [History of Educational Technology Resistance](https://www.oecd-ilibrary.org/education/students-computers-and-learning_9789264239555-en) - OECD - International data on education's historically slow technology adoption, confirming that the Ostrich Academy is less fictional than comfortable.
